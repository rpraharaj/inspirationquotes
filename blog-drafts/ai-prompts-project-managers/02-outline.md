### H2: Common Mistakes Project Managers Make with AI (And How to Avoid Them)
**Words:** 400-450
**Goal:** Warn against pitfalls; build trust through honest advice

**Mistakes:**
1. **Blindly Trusting AI Outputs**
   - Example: AI risk assessment that missed regulatory compliance
   - Fix: Always validate against your expertise
   
2. **Using Vague Prompts**
   - Bad: "Create a project plan"
   - Good: "Create a 3-month project plan for migrating 500 users to new CRM, budget $50K, team of 4 developers"
   
3. **Over-Relying on AI for Human Decisions**
   - AI can't assess team morale, political dynamics, or soft skills
   - Use AI for prep, not replacement of judgment
   
4. **Ignoring Data Privacy**
   - Don't paste confidential client data into ChatGPT/Claude
   - Use enterprise versions or anonymize data
   
5. **Not Iterating on Prompts**
   - First output is draft, not final
   - Refine, add context, re-prompt

**Opinion:** "If you're not questioning AI outputs, you're doing PM wrong."

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions
**Words:** 350-400
**Goal:** Capture PAA traffic, answer common concerns

**Questions:**

#### Q1: Will AI replace project managers?
**Answer:** No. AI will replace PMs who don't use AI, but not the role itself. Project management requires human judgment, empathy, stakeholder navigation, and leadership - skills AI lacks. AI handles repetitive tasks (status updates, timelines) so you can focus on strategy and people management. Think of AI as your chief of staff, not your replacement.

#### Q2: Which AI tool is best for project management - ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini?
**Answer:** It depends on the task. Claude 4 Opus excels at complex reasoning (risk assessment, dependencies), GPT-5 is best for clear communication (emails, reports), and Gemini 3 Pro integrates well with Google Workspace. I use all three: Claude for planning, ChatGPT for daily tasks, Gemini for documentation. Start with ChatGPT (most user-friendly) and expand from there.

#### Q3: How do I make AI-generated project plans more reliable?
**Answer:** Be specific in your prompts. Include project scope, team size, budget, constraints, and methodology (Agile vs. Waterfall). Always validate AI outputs against your expertise - AI doesn't know your team's capacity, organizational politics, or hidden risks. Treat AI as a first draft that requires your refinement, not a final answer.

#### Q4: Can AI help with Agile/Scrum project management specifically?
**Answer:** Yes! AI is excellent for sprint planning, user story generation, retrospective preparation, and backlog refinement. Use prompts that specify Scrum terminology (e.g., "Create sprint backlog for 2-week sprint with 5 developers, velocity 40 story points"). However, AI can't facilitate retrospectives or assess team dynamics - that's your job as Scrum Master or PM.

#### Q5: What are the biggest mistakes project managers make with AI?
**Answer:** (1) Blindly trusting AI outputs without validation, (2) Using vague prompts that produce generic results, (3) Over-relying on AI for human decisions like team morale or stakeholder conflicts, (4) Pasting confidential data into public AI tools, (5) Not iterating on prompts - first output is rarely perfect. Always question AI, refine prompts, and apply your PM expertise.

#### Q6: How much time can AI actually save in project management?
**Answer:** Based on my experience and industry reports, AI can save 30-50% of time on administrative tasks like status updates, meeting notes, timeline creation, and report generation. For example, I cut sprint planning from 3 hours to 90 minutes using AI. However, AI doesn't save time on high-value PM work like stakeholder management or strategic decision-making - it frees you to focus on those areas.

#### Q7: Is it safe to use AI for project management with confidential data?
**Answer:** Use caution. Public AI tools (free ChatGPT, Claude) may use your inputs for training. For confidential projects, use enterprise versions (ChatGPT Plus Team/Enterprise, Claude for Business) that don't train on your data, or anonymize sensitive information before prompting. Never paste client names, proprietary data, or regulated information into public AI without proper data handling policies.
