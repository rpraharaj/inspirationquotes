## How to Write Effective ChatGPT Prompts

Before we dive into the 100 prompts, let me share what actually makes a prompt work. I've learned this the hard way—through countless frustrating responses that missed the mark entirely.

### The Anatomy of a Great Prompt

Every effective prompt has five key components:

1. **Context** – Background information ChatGPT needs to understand your situation
2. **Role** – Who ChatGPT should "act as" (expert, coach, analyst)
3. **Task** – The specific thing you want accomplished
4. **Format** – How you want the output structured (bullets, paragraphs, table)
5. **Constraints** – Limits on length, tone, or approach

You don't need all five every time, but the more specific you are, the better your results.

### GPT-5 Changes the Game

Here's my honest take: GPT-5.2 actually requires *simpler* prompts than earlier models. The reasoning is stronger, so you don't need to over-explain. The context window is massive (272K tokens), so you can paste entire documents without trimming. And the model follows instructions more reliably.

That said, specificity still wins. "Write me something good" will still give you something generic.

## Writing & Content Creation Prompts

I use these prompts almost daily. The difference between a mediocre AI-generated article and a genuinely useful one comes down to how you set up the prompt. Here are my 10 favorites for writing.

### 1. Blog Post Writer

```
Act as an expert content writer who specializes in [your niche]. Write a 1,500-word blog post about [topic].

Requirements:
- Start with a compelling hook (story, surprising fact, or bold statement—NOT "In this article...")
- Include 5 practical tips with real-world examples
- Add a FAQ section with 3 common questions
- End with a clear call-to-action

Tone: Professional but conversational, like explaining to a smart friend
Audience: [describe your target reader]
```

**Customize it:** Replace [your niche] with your industry, and [topic] with your specific subject.

### 2. Headline Generator

```
Generate 15 headline variations for an article about [topic].

Include:
- 5 curiosity-driven headlines
- 5 benefit-focused headlines
- 5 with numbers/data

Target audience: [who will read this]
Avoid: Clickbait, all caps, overused phrases like "you won't believe"
```

### 3. Article Outline Creator

```
Create a detailed outline for a [word count]-word article about [topic].

For each section, include:
- H2 heading
- 2-3 H3 subheadings
- Key points to cover (3-5 bullets)
- Estimated word count

Target keyword: [primary keyword]
Audience: [beginner/intermediate/expert]
```

### 4. Content Rewriter (Tone Shift)

```
Rewrite the following content in a [new tone] voice while keeping all the key information intact:

[paste your content]

Original tone: [current tone]
Target tone: [desired tone—e.g., formal to casual, technical to accessible]
Preserve: Key facts, data, and main arguments
Change: Sentence structure, vocabulary, personality
```

### 5. Email Newsletter Writer

```
Write a weekly newsletter email for [brand/company] about [topic/theme].

Structure:
- Subject line (under 50 characters)
- Preview text (under 90 characters)
- Opening hook
- 3 main points or updates
- One call-to-action
- Brief sign-off

Tone: [brand voice]
Length: ~400 words
```

### 6. Product Description Creator

```
Write a compelling product description for [product name].

Product details:
- Category: [type]
- Key features: [list 3-5]
- Target customer: [who]
- Price point: [budget/mid-range/premium]

Include:
- Emotional hook in the opening line
- Feature-benefit pairings
- Sensory language where relevant
- Subtle urgency without being pushy

Length: 150-200 words
```

### 7. Social Proof Copy Generator

```
Transform these customer reviews/testimonials into polished marketing copy:

[paste reviews]

Create:
- 3 shortened quotes (under 30 words each)
- 1 case study summary (100 words)
- 3 social proof statistics ("X customers love...")

Keep: Authentic voice and specific details
Remove: Grammatical errors, rambling
```

### 8. SEO Content Optimizer

```
Analyze and optimize this content for the keyword [target keyword]:

[paste content]

Provide:
- Current keyword usage assessment
- Recommended keyword placements (naturally)
- Missing LSI keywords to add
- Meta description (under 160 characters)
- 3 internal linking opportunities
- Readability improvements
```

### 9. Proofreader & Editor

```
Proofread and edit the following content:

[paste content]

Check for:
- Grammar and spelling errors
- Passive voice (highlight and suggest active alternatives)
- Run-on sentences
- Unclear phrasing
- Tone consistency

Format your feedback as:
1. Corrected version
2. List of changes made
3. Suggestions for improvement
```

### 10. Storytelling Hook Creator

```
Generate 5 different opening hooks for an article about [topic]:

1. Personal story angle
2. Surprising statistic
3. Provocative question
4. Bold contrarian statement
5. Vivid scene-setting

Each hook should be 2-3 sentences and make the reader want to continue.
Target audience: [who]
```

**Pro tip for writing prompts:** Always specify what you don't want. Telling ChatGPT "don't start with 'In this article'" prevents the most common AI writing cliche.

## Coding & Development Prompts

I'll admit—debugging with ChatGPT changed my life. I spent three hours on a bug last month that GPT-5 solved in 30 seconds. Here are the prompts that make that magic happen.

### 11. Code Generator

```
Write a [language] function that [describes what it should do].

Requirements:
- Input: [describe input parameters]
- Output: [describe expected return]
- Include error handling for [edge cases]
- Add comments explaining the logic
- Follow [style guide—e.g., PEP 8, Airbnb]

Example usage should be included at the bottom.
```

### 12. Bug Finder & Fixer

```
Debug this code and explain what's wrong:

[paste your buggy code]

For each issue found:
1. Identify the exact line(s) with the problem
2. Explain WHY it's causing issues
3. Provide the corrected code
4. Explain the fix

Also check for:
- Logic errors
- Edge cases that might break it
- Performance issues
- Security vulnerabilities
```

### 13. Code Explainer (Line-by-Line)

```
Explain this code like I'm a [beginner/intermediate/expert] developer:

[paste code]

Provide:
- High-level summary of what it does (2-3 sentences)
- Line-by-line explanation
- Key concepts someone should understand
- Potential improvements or modern alternatives
```

### 14. Refactoring Assistant

```
Refactor this code to improve [readability/performance/maintainability]:

[paste code]

Current issues: [what you think is wrong]
Language: [programming language]
Target style: [functional/OOP/other]

Provide:
- Refactored version
- Explanation of each change
- Any trade-offs to consider
```

### 15. Unit Test Writer

```
Write comprehensive unit tests for this function:

[paste function]

Testing framework: [Jest/Pytest/Mocha/other]

Include tests for:
- Happy path (expected inputs)
- Edge cases (empty, null, extreme values)
- Error conditions
- Boundary values

Each test should have a clear description of what it's testing.
```

### 16. API Documentation Generator

```
Generate API documentation for this endpoint/function:

[paste code or describe endpoint]

Include:
- Endpoint/function description
- Parameters (name, type, required/optional, description)
- Request example (with headers if relevant)
- Response example (success and error cases)
- Error codes and meanings
- Usage notes
```

### 17. Algorithm Implementer

```
Implement [algorithm name] in [language].

Requirements:
- Include the algorithm from scratch (don't use built-in library versions)
- Add comments explaining each step
- Analyze time and space complexity
- Provide example usage with sample input/output
- Include edge case handling
```

### 18. Code Reviewer

```
Review this code as a senior developer would:

[paste code]

Evaluate:
- Code quality and readability
- Best practices adherence
- Potential bugs
- Security concerns
- Performance optimizations
- Suggestions for improvement

Format: Prioritized list from most to least important issues.
```

### 19. Error Message Decoder

```
I'm getting this error:

[paste error message and stack trace]

My code:
[paste relevant code]

Help me:
1. Understand what this error means in plain English
2. Identify the likely cause
3. Provide step-by-step fix instructions
4. Explain how to prevent this in the future
```

### 20. Tech Stack Advisor

```
I'm building [describe your project].

Requirements:
- Scale: [expected users/traffic]
- Team size: [number of developers]
- Budget: [bootstrap/funded/enterprise]
- Timeline: [how soon to launch]
- Key features: [list main features]

Recommend:
- Frontend framework
- Backend language/framework
- Database choice
- Hosting/infrastructure
- Key third-party services

For each, explain WHY it fits my needs (not just what's popular).
```

**My hot take on coding prompts:** Paste more context than you think you need. With GPT-5's expanded context window, entire files work better than snippets.

## Tips for Maximizing Your Prompt Results

After testing hundreds of prompts, here's what I've learned about getting consistently great results.

### Iterate, Don't Settle

Your first response isn't always the best. Try:
- "Make this more concise"
- "Give me 5 alternatives"
- "What did you miss?"
- "Try a different approach"

The magic often happens on the second or third refinement.

### Chain Your Prompts

Complex tasks work better as a sequence: Use the output from one prompt as input to another. For example:
1. First prompt: Research and outline
2. Second prompt: Write the draft
3. Third prompt: Edit and polish

This is how I wrote most of this article.

### Save Your Best Prompts

When you find a prompt that works perfectly, save it. I keep a [custom GPT](/blog/create-custom-gpt-guide) with my favorites for instant access.

### Customize, Don't Just Copy

Every prompt in this list is a template. The best results come when you adapt them to your specific context, audience, and goals.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What makes a ChatGPT prompt "good"?

A good prompt is clear, specific, and provides context. Instead of "write me an email," you'd say "write a friendly reminder email to a client who missed our meeting, keeping it under 100 words and suggesting three alternative times." Specificity is the secret.

### Do longer prompts get better results?

Not necessarily. Clarity matters more than length. With GPT-5.2, shorter prompts often work better because the model understands nuance more reliably. That said, don't sacrifice important context just to be brief.

### Can I use these prompts with Claude, Gemini, or other AI tools?

Yes! Most prompts work across major LLMs. You may need minor adjustments—Claude tends to be more verbose, Gemini might need format tweaks—but the core structure transfers well.

### How do I know if a prompt is working?

Run it multiple times and check for consistency. A good prompt gives you reliably useful output, not just one lucky response. If results vary wildly, your prompt needs more specificity.

### Should I always use role-based prompts ("Act as...")?

Often, yes. Assigning a role (expert copywriter, senior developer, business analyst) helps ChatGPT adopt the right perspective, vocabulary, and approach. It's one of the simplest ways to improve output quality.
