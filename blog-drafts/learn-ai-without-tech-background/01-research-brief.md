## 2. Key Research Findings

### Core Principles for Non-Technical AI Learning

1. **Focus on Practical Application** - What AI does, not how it's built
2. **Start Small and Be Curious** - One tool, one use case at a time
3. **Embrace No-Code Tools** - Visual interfaces, drag-and-drop, pre-built components
4. **Understand Core Concepts** - AI, ML, deep learning at a conceptual level
5. **Don't Fear the Math** - You don't need it for practical AI usage

### Recommended Learning Paths

**Phase 1: Foundational Understanding**
- What AI is vs. ML vs. deep learning (conceptual)
- Key terminology (neural networks, NLP, etc.)
- Capabilities and limitations
- Real-world applications
- Ethical considerations

**Phase 2: Practical Application (No-Code)**
- Prompt engineering as entry point
- Generative AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude)
- No-code platforms (Lobe, Bubble, Zapier)
- AI-powered analytics

**Phase 3: Domain-Specific Application**
- Identify AI opportunities in your field
- Industry-specific use cases
- Continuous learning

## 5. Unique Angle

**Thesis:** You don't need to become a programmer to become AI-proficient. The most valuable AI skill for non-technical people is learning to APPLY AI effectively, not build it.

**Key Messages:**
1. Technical background not required - millions learning AI without coding
2. Prompt engineering is the great equalizer
3. AI literacy matters more than AI engineering for most roles
4. Focus on what AI can do for YOUR work, not AI generally
5. The bar has never been lower - tools are designed for non-technical users
