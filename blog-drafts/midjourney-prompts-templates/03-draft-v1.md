## How to Use These Prompt Templates

Before we dive into the templates, let's make sure you get the most out of them.

### The Anatomy of a Great Midjourney Prompt

Effective Midjourney prompts follow a consistent structure:

**Subject + Environment + Lighting + Style + Technical Parameters**

For example:
- Subject: "A weathered fisherman with deep wrinkles"
- Environment: "on a wooden dock at dawn"
- Lighting: "warm golden hour light, rim lighting on hair"
- Style: "portrait photography, candid moment"
- Parameters: "--ar 2:3 --style raw"

### Essential Parameters You'll See

- **--ar [ratio]**: Aspect ratio (16:9, 2:3, 1:1, etc.)
- **--style raw**: Removes Midjourney's default artistic styling for more photorealistic results
- **--stylize [0-1000]**: Lower numbers follow your prompt more closely; higher numbers allow more artistic freedom
- **--no [element]**: Excludes unwanted elements

### Customizing the Templates

Throughout these templates, you'll see [BRACKETS] indicating elements you should replace:
- [SUBJECT] = Person, object, or scene description
- [ENVIRONMENT] = Location or setting
- [DETAILS] = Specific characteristics
- [COLOR] = Color choices
- [STYLE] = Artistic reference

## Pro Tips for Better Results

After testing hundreds of prompts, here's what I've learned:

### Prompt Length Sweet Spot

Midjourney works best with prompts between 40-75 words. Too short and you lose control. Too long and the AI gets confused. The templates above hit this sweet spot.

### When to Use --style raw

Use `--style raw` when you want:
- Photorealistic results
- Less "AI art" aesthetic
- Images that look like actual photographs

Skip it when you want:
- More artistic interpretation
- Stylized illustration
- Creative surprises

### Iterating and Refining

The first result is rarely the best. I typically:
1. Generate 4 images from the initial prompt
2. Upscale promising options
3. Use "vary subtle" or "vary strong" on favorites
4. Note what worked for future prompts

### Combining Template Elements

These templates are starting points. Mix elements from different categories:
- Lighting from portrait templates + subject from product templates
- Mood from noir + setting from cyberpunk
- Technical settings from landscape + composition from architecture

Experimentation is how you develop your own signature style.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best aspect ratio for portraits?

For vertical portraits, use `--ar 2:3` or `--ar 4:5`. For headshots, `--ar 2:3` works well. For full-body, consider `--ar 3:4` or even `--ar 9:16` for social media.

### How do I get more realistic results?

Add `--style raw` to remove Midjourney's artistic styling. Include camera and lens specifications in your prompt. Reference real photography techniques like lighting setups and film stocks.

### Can I combine multiple prompts?

Yes, use the `::` syntax to weight different concepts. For example: `portrait photography:: moody lighting::2 warm colors::0.5` gives double weight to moody lighting.

### What's the difference between --stylize values?

- 0-100: Very literal interpretation of your prompt
- 100-400: Balanced creativity and prompt adherence
- 400-1000: Maximum artistic freedom, may drift from prompt

For these templates, I recommend 0-300 for most uses, higher for abstract/artistic work.
