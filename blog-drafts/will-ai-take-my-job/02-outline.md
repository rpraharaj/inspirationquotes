### H2: What the Data Actually Says (The Research Is Complicated) (600 words)

**Central tension:** Why predictions vary wildly - and what to make of that

#### H3: The Job Displacement Numbers

**Goldman Sachs findings:**
- 6-7% of US workforce could be displaced
- 25 million full-time job equivalents by 2026
- But: "modest and temporary" impact expected
- Productivity boost: 15% in developed markets

**McKinsey findings:**
- 57% of US work hours *could* theoretically be automated
- 40% of jobs in "highly automatable" categories
- But: This is technical potential, not prediction
- Reality: Jobs evolve, don't disappear entirely

**World Economic Forum:**
- Initial: 97M jobs created vs. 85M displaced (net +12M)
- Updated for 2027: 83M lost vs. 69M created (net -14M)
- Contradictory? Different timeframes, different methodologies

#### H3: Why Experts Disagree So Much

- Measuring "jobs" vs. "tasks" vs. "hours"
- Pace of adoption unknowable
- Historical precedent mixed
- **Honest admission:** Nobody really knows

### H2: Your Personal Job Risk Assessment Framework (400 words)

**Unique differentiator - actionable self-evaluation tool**

#### H3: The 5 Questions to Ask About Your Job

**Question 1: How repetitive is your work?**
- High repetition = higher risk
- Variable tasks = lower risk

**Question 2: How structured are your decisions?**
- Clear rules = AI can learn them
- Judgment calls = harder to automate

**Question 3: How much human interaction is core?**
- Transactional = at risk
- Relationship-based = safer

**Question 4: How physical is your environment?**
- Office/computer = higher automation potential
- Variable physical = lower

**Question 5: How quickly is AI advancing in your field?**
- Already disrupting = act now
- Still early = more time

#### H3: Scoring Your Risk Level

**Simple framework:**
- 4-5 high-risk answers = High risk
- 2-3 high-risk answers = Moderate risk
- 0-1 high-risk answers = Lower risk

**Callout box:** "Be honest with yourself. Denial doesn't protect careers."

### H2: FAQ (300 words)

#### H3: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How soon will AI significantly impact jobs?**
A: It's already happening in some sectors. Most impact expected 2025-2030, with pace varying by industry.

**Q: Should I quit my job if it's high-risk?**
A: Not necessarily immediately, but start preparing now. Use your current role to build transferable skills while exploring options.

**Q: Is it too late to learn new skills at [age]?**
A: No. People are successfully pivoting at all ages. The key is consistent effort, not youth.

**Q: Will AI eventually take ALL jobs?**
A: Unlikely in any near-term scenario. Human creativity, empathy, and judgment remain difficult to replicate. Jobs will change; work won't disappear.

**Q: What if I don't want to work with AI?**
A: That choice will increasingly limit your options. Even AI-resistant jobs will involve AI tools. Resistance isn't a strategy.

**Q: Which industries are safest from AI?**
A: Healthcare, skilled trades, education, and social services have more human-centered elements. But even these will integrate AI.
