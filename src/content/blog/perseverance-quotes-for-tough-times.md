Last year, I hit my lowest point. A project I'd poured my heart into for two years fell apart in a single afternoon. I sat in my car for an hour, staring at the steering wheel, wondering if I had the strength to start over. Then my phone buzzed with a text from an old friend: "It's not about how many times you get knocked down. It's about that one time you get up and stay up." I didn't feel inspired in that moment, but I did feel seen. And somehow, that was enough to get me through the day.

But they also don't last forever, even when it feels like they will. What gets us through isn't superhuman strength or blind optimism. It's perseverance: the quiet, stubborn refusal to give up even when giving up seems like the only option.
