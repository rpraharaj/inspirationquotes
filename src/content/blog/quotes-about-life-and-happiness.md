I used to think that happiness was a destination—a place I would finally arrive at once I checked enough boxes on my life's to-do list. I thought I'd be happy when I got the job, when I bought the house, or when I finally had "enough" in the bank. But life has a funny way of teaching us that happiness isn't at the finish line; it's the rhythm of the walk itself.

There's something incredibly grounding about finding the right words when you're feeling a bit lost. For me, a single sentence can often do more than a whole self-help book. It's like an espresso shot of wisdom that wakes up my perspective. That's why I've gathered these words—they've been my companions on days when the sun felt bright and on nights when I needed a reminder that the dawn was coming.
